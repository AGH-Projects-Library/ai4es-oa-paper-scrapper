## 3.1 Loss Prediction

 The final loss of properly-trained large language models is thought to be well approximated by power laws in the amount of compute used to train the model [ 41 , 42 , 2 , 14 , 15 ] . To verify the scalability of our optimization infrastructure, we predicted GPT-4’s final loss on our internal codebase (not part of the training set) by fitting a scaling law with an irreducible loss term (as in Henighan et al. [ 15 ] ): L ​ ( C ) = a ​ C b + c , 𝐿 𝐶 𝑎 superscript 𝐶 𝑏 𝑐 L(C)=aC^{b}+c, from models trained using the same methodology but using at most 10,000x less compute than GPT-4. This prediction was made shortly after the run started, without use of any partial results.
