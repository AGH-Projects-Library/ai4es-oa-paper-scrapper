 To demonstrate the application of Signal Temporal Logic to the heart model, we design four critical policies that capture essential safety and performance requirements for cardiac electrical activity. These specifications illustrate the types of temporal properties that must be verified in digital healthcare systems. The first policy ensures proper atrioventricular conduction timing, which is critical for maintaining coordinated heart rhythm: [[TABLE_0]]  This specification requires that whenever atrial activation occurs ( A E тАЛ G тАЛ M A_{EGM} exceeds threshold V a , t тАЛ h V_{a,th} ), ventricular activation must follow within 180ms to 240ms ( V E тАЛ G тАЛ M V_{EGM} exceeds threshold V v , t тАЛ h V_{v,th} ). This property ensures that the AV node conducts electrical impulses from the atria to the ventricles properly, preventing dangerous delays. The second policy enforces the ventricular refractory period, preventing premature ventricular contractions that could lead to arrhythmias: [[TABLE_1]]  This specification ensures that after each ventricular activation, the ventricular myocardium enters the refractory region ( V E тАЛ G тАЛ M V_{EGM} below threshold) within 600ms to 1000ms after the ventricular activation. This property prevents the occurrence of dangerous rapid ventricular rhythms and ensures adequate time for ventricular filling. The fourth property ensures that the heart model never deadlocks. It will eventually issue an atrial activation and a ventricular activation.
