## 5.4 Patient Pre-Context and Personalisation

 A key component of our framework is the use of optional patient pre-context before the dialogue begins. This pre-context may include demographic or clinically useful attributes such as age, gender, height, weight, allergies, and other basic history fields. We prepend this information to the conversation as a structured consultation profile, allowing the model to condition its questioning strategy on essential patient characteristics. This design more closely matches real consultation settings, where physicians often begin with basic contextual information before exploring symptoms in detail. It also enables more personalized follow-up questions, especially in cases where age, sex, or allergy information may influence diagnostic reasoning.
