 We retrospectively queried all patients from birth through 18 years of age with double outlet right ventricle (DORV) that underwent biventricular repair with an intracardiac left ventricular (LV) to aortic baffle at Lucille Packard Children’s Hospital from 2018-2025. Exclusion criteria included insufficient imaging data (pre-operative computed tomography angiogram (CTA)), diagnosis of levo-transposition of the great arteries, and operative VSD enlargement. Cases involving VSD enlargement were excluded because the current framework does not model or optimize VSD enlargement, and including such cases would limit direct comparison between the planned repair and the actual postoperative anatomy and outcomes. A cohort of four patients was identified for preliminary validation (Table 1 ).
