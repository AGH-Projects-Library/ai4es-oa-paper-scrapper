## 3.1 Final baffle surfaces and simulation-ready meshes

 The workflow was successfully applied to all four DORV patient cases, producing high-fidelity, patient-specific surface models and simulation-ready computational meshes for each case. Across the cohort, the constructed baffles formed smooth, continuous surfaces that were free of self-intersections and integrate seamlessly with the native anatomy at the attachment boundary. In addition to meeting the prescribed geometric constraints, the resulting domains were watertight and topologically consistent, enabling robust downstream meshing and hemodynamic simulation.
