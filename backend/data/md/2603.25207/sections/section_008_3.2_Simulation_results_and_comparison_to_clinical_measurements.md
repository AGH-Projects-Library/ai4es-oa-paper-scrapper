 Steady-state CFD simulations were performed on all final baffle models to analyze the post-operative flow and pressure environment. For each of the four DORV patients, the converged solution at the final timestep was visualized using streamline trajectories seeded at the VSD inlet. Across all patients, the streamlines demonstrated coherent inlet-to-outlet transport through the intraventricular tunnel. Flow entering from the VSD was redirected toward the aortic outlet, with streamline trajectories that followed the baffle geometry and no evidence of large-scale flow reversal. These streamlines can be visualized in the first two rows of the figure below. These patterns suggest that the baffle geometry successfully guides ventricular flow toward the systemic outlet. We also evaluated the pressure field along the baffle pathway by reporting the converged steady-state pressures of the inlet and outlet. Specifically, P inlet \displaystyle P_{\text{inlet}} and P outlet \displaystyle P_{\text{outlet}} were computed as the converged, area-averaged static pressures on the VSD inlet and aortic outlet faces, respectively. These values represent mean face pressures for the converged solution rather than pulsatile peak gradients. Localized pressure variations were observed along the baffle surface due to curvature and local flow acceleration; however, the inlet and outlet pressures provide a global characterization of the pressure environment associated with baffle-guided flow. Across patients, the resulting pressure drops ranged between 2 − 5 \displaystyle 2-5 mmHg. These relatively small pressure drops suggest that the baffle pathway introduced only a modest additional resistance to the ventricular outflow.
